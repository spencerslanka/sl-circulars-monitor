Granting entitlement to benefits to the members of Police or Special Task Force as per the 
Cabinet Approval No. CP/19/2618/103/069 - I dated 24.09.2019 
 
 
After making the Cabinet of Ministers aware of the Sub Paragraphs I and II below by the Note to 
the Cabinet dated 02.08.2023 relevant to the Cabinet decision No: CP/19/2618/103/069-I dated 
24.09.2019, concurrence has been granted at the meeting of the Cabinet of Ministers held on 
04.09.2023 to take action in the manner indicated in the said Sub Paragraphs.    
 
I. 
As per Section 14 of the Pensions Minute, only the Public Officers who are below the age 
of 55 years of age are sent on retirement on recommendation of a Medical Board. The 
members of Police or Special Task Force, who are entitled to this allowance, are retiring 
during the age between 55 to 60 years.      
 
II. 
A circular is issued by the Secretary of the Ministry of Public Administration to pay the 
fixed monthly allowance by Sri Lanka Police to the members of Police or Special Task 
Force, who are entitled as mentioned in para 02 of the Circular of the Ministry of Defense 
No: MOD/01/2020 and sent on retirement at the age of 55 years or between 55- 60 years 
of age under Section 2:17 of the Pensions Minute, subject to the provisions of the Cabinet 
approval dated 24.09.2019.   
 
2. Further, approval has been granted at the meeting of the Cabinet of Ministers held on 
19.02.2025 to take action in the above manner. 
 
3. Provisions have been included in the circular of the Ministry of Defense No: MOD/01/2020  
dated 15.07.2020, which was issued as per the approval of the Cabinet of Ministers dated 
24.09.2019, to pay the difference between the pension entitled at the date of retirement and the 
      Public Administration Circular Letter : 01/2025

aggregate of both the last drawn salary and allowances as a fixed monthly allowance by the Three 
Armed Forces or Police to the members of Three Armed Forces, Police and Special Task Force, 
who have become disabled due to their direct engagement in the military operations during the 
war situation that existed before 19.05.2009 or their contribution to post-war activities, which 
have been carried out as  the result of the previous war. 
  
4. Action should be taken subject to the following provisions when members of Police or 
Special Task Force are granted entitlement to the benefits mentioned in the circular of the 
Ministry of Defense No: MOD/01/2020 as per the Cabinet Decision No CP/19/2618/103/069-I 
dated 24.09.2019.   
4.1 To pay this fixed monthly allowance to the members of Police or Special Task Force, 
who are entitled as mentioned in para 02 of the Circular of the Ministry of Defense No: 
MOD/01/2020, by Sri Lanka Police as per Section 03 of the circular.   . 
 
4.2 Those who have become disabled due to their direct engagement in military operations 
before 19.05.2009 and  are entitled under paragraph 02 of the circular of the Ministry of 
Defense No: MOD/01/2020;  
 
(a) 
Members of Police or Special Task Force of over the age of 55 years , whose 
percentage of disability, on which the Medical board has recommended that the 
person is not fit for the service further after making an examination after 
15.07.2020, the date of the Circular, is 20% or more but sent on retirement under 
Section 2:17 of the Pensions Minute are entitled to this benefit.  
 
(b) 
The members of Police or Special Task Force, who are in service at present, are 
given an opportunity to appear before a Medical Board 06 month prior to their 
date of retirement and those who are sent on retirement under Section 2:17 of the 
Pensions Minute even where the Medical Board recommends that their 
percentage of disability is 20% or above, are entitled to this benefit.      
 
4.3 Above mentioned medical examination should be conducted by a special Medical Board 
appointed by the Director General of Health Services. 
 
4.4 Only the officers out of those indicated in the name list already recommended by the 
Ministry of Public Security, who have satisfied prescribed qualifications, will be eligible 
to apply for the benefits as per the provisions of this Circular letter.
