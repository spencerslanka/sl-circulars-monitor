Establishment of a New Insurance Scheme for Public Officers Proposed  
in the Budget 2026 (Agrahara) 
 
Your attention is drawn to Public Administration Circular 12/2005 dated 18.05.2005 issued with 
regard to the above matter, and the consequent circulars issued. 
 
02. 
With the objective of expanding the health benefit coverage of the Agrahara insurance 
scheme and to ensure the stability of benefits provided to public officers, it was decided at the 
Cabinet meeting held on 09.02.2026 to amend the existing provisions and include new provisions 
as follows. 
 
03. 
Accordingly, the existing provisions will be amended and new provisions will be included 
as follows.

II. 
In instances where both the policyholder and his/ her spouse are 
public officers but are contributors to different insurance 
schemes, provide the opportunity to apply for common benefits 
(those applicable to the spouse and dependents) through the 
policyholder entitled to the higher benefits, excluding the benefits 
specifically granted only for the primary policyholder. 
 
4. 
10 
In the event that an Agrahara member passes away while hospitalized, 
medical claims incurred prior to the officer's death can be obtained as 
follows:  
I. 
If the officer was married, the claim can be made by the spouse or 
one of the children (subject to the consent of the other party).  
 
II. If the officer was unmarried, the claim can be made by one of the 
parents, either the mother or the father (subject to the consent of the 
other party).   
 
5. 
11 
The relevant bills must be received by the National Insurance Trust Fund 
within 90 days from the date of the bills or from the date the officer was 
discharged from the hospital. 
 
I. 
Applications sent via registered post will be considered if they have 
been registered within 90 days. 
 
II. 
Applications sent by ordinary post or delivered by hand, must be 
received by the National Insurance Trust Fund within 90 days. 
 
III. 
In the event of the death of an Agrahara member, the death claim 
file must be submitted to the National Insurance Trust Fund 
through the Head of the Institution by the claimants within 06 
months from the date of death of the member. 
 
6. 
12 
It is the responsibility of the Head of the Institution to forward the 
applications submitted by the Agrahara member to their respective Head 
of Institution within the stipulated timeframe to the National Insurance 
Trust Fund before the due date. Furthermore, the Head of the Institution 
must ensure that an appropriate system is established for this purpose. 
 
04. 
All other provisions included in Public Administration Circular 12/2005 dated 18.05.2005 
and the circulars issued consequently shall remain unchanged, along with the amended provisions 
specified in Table 3.1 and new provisions specified in Table 3.2 above.
